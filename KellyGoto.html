  • Siloed Information < Collaborative thinking, break it up!
  • you must be able pivot when changes/requests come in that will affect your project. This means being able to change in strategy while not taking away from quality
  • MVP = minimum viable product
  • When coming out with a product/design, you may get lost in your pile of features. The best thing to do is to focus most of your energy on the minimum elements that need to be awesome to help ensure a successful deployment
  • Physical/digital presence is important for quality collaborative work
  • Get your team together when you are working on a project. Is someone on ur team working from home or on the opposite coast? So what, send them a link to get Skype or a Google+ account. Have their faces up while you all work together, this will keep everyone motivated to focus on their work and stay collaborative

  • Responsive Design
  • When building for responsive websites, it is a good idea to build for the mobile first and build outward to the desktop site. This usually means you are starting with the leanest, healthiest content first and embellishing it outward for the desktop experience
  • a key to successful responsive design lies in the content. understanding how your content will move around the screen, according to the level of relevance(hierarchy) - this is content choreography
  • Lean UX Research
  • Its all about the culture of those you are testing
  • get someone like your customer, who thinks like your customer
  • get coffee cards.incentive for the people testing your dealio
  • the whole team needs to be included in the research and know all the feedback in order to put that work/research to use properly
  • Dont just SAY it, SHOW it - this is much more effective
  • Sprint Testing (Super Sets)
  • Concentrated and short sessions of quality work followed by an even shorter period to test & rest. Rinse and repeat.
  • 2-3 weeks of straight work, focusing only on the product. Then theres is a short period of testing (only 10 minutes are really needed for UI testing) and you can start all over
  • Low-Fi vs High-Fi (Fidelity)
  • low-fi:
  • quick mock-ups
  • good way to get a nice “proof of concept”
  • but is it enough to get good feedback?
  • hi-fi
  • clearer to digest for the client
  • nicer presentation of work
  • more time consuming
